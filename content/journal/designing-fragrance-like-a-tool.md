---
title: "Designing fragrance like a tool, not a story"
date: "2026-07-02"
excerpt: "Why we start every formulation with an outcome — focus, calm, energy — and work backward to the molecules, instead of starting with a mood board."
---

Most fragrance briefs start with a story: a place, a memory, a feeling to evoke. Ours start with an outcome — a state you want to get into, like focus or calm — and we work backward from there.

That means the first questions in any formulation session aren't "what does this smell like" but "what is this supposed to do, and what's the evidence it does it." Only once we have an answer do we start pulling raw materials.

It's a slower way to build a scent. It's also the only way we know of to end up with something that's more than a nice story in a bottle.
